## B. Reddit-safe comment templates (10)

**Rule of thumb:** answer the question fully and helpfully *first*; mention Parkio only when it genuinely fits, and not in every comment. Aim ~1 mention per 10 helpful replies. Never paste the same line repeatedly — reword.

1. **"Best way to check wait times?"**
> Honestly the official app works but it's slow to load and you have to dig. I keep a live color-coded view open instead so I can glance and go — parkio.info is the free one I use. Either way, the trick is checking *before* you commit to walking across the park.

2. **"Is [park] busy on [date]?"**
> Crowd calendars are educated guesses. The realest signal is opening-hour waits that morning — if headliners are already 45+ at rope drop, it's a heavy day. Plan to hit your top ride first regardless.

3. **"First Disney trip — any tips?"**
> Three that matter most: be at the gate before opening, ride your #1 headliner first, and check live waits before you pick your next ride (don't just walk to the nearest line). Everything else you can wing.

4. **"Worth paying to skip lines?"**
> Depends on your day length and which rides you care about. If you rope drop well and ride during parades/meals, you can do a lot on standby alone. Track the dips and you'll be surprised how much you skip for free.

5. **"How do I do rope drop right?"**
> Get there ~45 min before official open, be near the front, and go straight to the highest-demand ride first thing. The first 90 minutes are worth more than the next four hours combined.

6. **"Which park should we do on which day?"**
> Match parks to your group's must-dos, and avoid the park with early-entry hours that day if you're not a resort guest (it gets a head start of crowds). Then just ride early and around shows.

7. **"Single rider — worth it?"**
> 100% if you're okay splitting up for one ride. It's the most underused line-skip in the parks. Not every attraction has it, so check which ones do before you count on it.

8. **"Mobile order — when should I order?"**
> Order your slot an hour+ ahead, especially around lunch. The walk-up lines at noon are brutal; pre-ordering and riding during the lunch rush is the move.

9. **"How accurate are posted wait times?"**
> They're usually padded a bit (Disney would rather you be pleasantly surprised). Live trackers show the posted number; in practice you'll often wait a touch less, especially late in the day.

10. **"Best ride with the shortest wait?"**
> Depends on the park and time, but the well-themed secondary rides often sit near walk-on while everyone queues for the headliners. Glance at live waits and grab whatever's green near you.
